Pre-Workout

1.Hydration: Start your day with a glass of water and continue to hydrate throughout the day. 2.Balanced Meal: Consume a balanced meal 1-2 hours before your workout. 3.Include complex carbohydrates (oats, whole grains) for sustained energy. 4.Include lean protein (chicken, tofu) for muscle support. 5.Add healthy fats (avocado, nuts) for satiety and energy.

During Workout:

1.Hydration: Sip water during your workout to stay hydrated, especially if it's intense or long. 2.Electrolytes: Consider electrolyte-rich drinks for longer workouts to replenish minerals lost through sweat.

Post-Workout:

1.Protein Intake: Consume a protein-rich meal or snack within 1-2 hours post-workout. 1.Helps in muscle recovery and growth. 2.Options: protein shake, chicken with vegetables, Greek yogurt with fruits. 3.Carbohydrates: Include complex carbs to replenish glycogen stores. 4.Whole grains, sweet potatoes, fruits. 5.Hydration: Drink water to rehydrate and aid in recovery.

Daily Nutrition:

1.Protein-Rich Diet: Ensure adequate protein intake throughout the day to support muscle maintenance and growth. 2.Complex Carbs: Choose whole grains over refined carbs for sustained energy. 3.Healthy Fats: Incorporate sources like avocados, nuts, and olive oil for overall health. 4.Vegetables and Fruits: Include a variety of colorful vegetables and fruits for vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants. 5.Moderation: Control portion sizes to avoid excess calories.

Snacking:

1.Protein Snacks: Opt for protein-rich snacks like Greek yogurt, cottage cheese, or a handful of nuts. 2.Fruits: Fresh fruits can provide quick energy and essential nutrients. 3.Hydration: Drink water regularly, even between meals.

Supplements:

1.Consult a Professional: Before taking any supplements, consult a healthcare provider or a registered dietitian. 2.Common Supplements: Protein powder, creatine, BCAAs, and multivitamins can be considered, if needed.
